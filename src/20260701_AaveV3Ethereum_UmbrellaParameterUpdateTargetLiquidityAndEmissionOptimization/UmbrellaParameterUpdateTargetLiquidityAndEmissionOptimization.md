## Simple Summary

This AIP updates the Umbrella Target Liquidity and emission configurations for the USDC, USDT, and WETH markets, and sunsets the GHO Umbrella market, transitioning responsibility for any future GHO deficits to the DAO Treasury.

The proposed configuration reduces annual incentive expenditure from $8.19 million to $3.35 million, representing annual savings of approximately $4.84 million (59%), while preserving meaningful coverage and maintaining competitive yields for Umbrella underwriters.

## Motivation

The initial configuration of Umbrella Target Liquidity and emission levels was set based on the risk profile, market conditions, and borrowing activity observed at launch. Since then, several drivers of Umbrella's risk framework have evolved materially:

- **Active loan exposure declined.** Aggregate borrowed amounts across the ETH, USDT, USDC, and GHO markets fell from approximately $11.8 billion to $6.8 billion, a reduction of roughly 43%.
- **Collateral quality improved across the stablecoin markets.** The share of debt backed by pristine collateral (ETH, WBTC, cbBTC, wstETH, USDC, USDT) increased from 72.5% to 86.1% for USDT and from 81.1% to 88.2% for USDC.
- **Deficit Offsets increased substantially.** The USDC Deficit Offset grew from 100,000 to 1.3 million USDC and the USDT Deficit Offset from 100,000 to 1.6 million USDT, providing a significantly larger DAO-funded first-loss buffer before Umbrella liquidity is exposed.
- **The relative yield environment compressed.** Risk-free rates, Aave supply yields, and competing DeFi yields have all declined since launch, reducing the opportunity cost of providing coverage and the emissions required to retain it.

For WETH, the target Umbrella APY is increased despite the reduction in Target Liquidity, to attract additional coverage to a market that has historically been underserved. For GHO, borrowing has declined to roughly $100 million; maintaining a dedicated Umbrella reserve and ongoing emissions is no longer the most efficient protection mechanism, so the market is wound down with the DAO Treasury absorbing any future deficits.

## Specification

The payload performs the following actions on Ethereum mainnet via the `UmbrellaConfigEngine`:

| Market (staked asset) |     New Target Liquidity |   New Emission Budget |
| --------------------- | -----------------------: | --------------------: |
| stkwaEthUSDT          |     34,114,525 waEthUSDT | 1.28M aEthUSDT / year |
| stkwaEthUSDC          |     32,518,555 waEthUSDC | 1.22M aEthUSDC / year |
| stkwaEthWETH          |      17,437.44 waEthWETH |   470 aEthWETH / year |
| stkGHO                | 1 GHO (protocol minimum) |   0 (reward disabled) |

- Annual emission budgets are converted on-chain to a per-second rate over a 365-day year, consistent with how the current live emissions were configured. The reward payer (`AaveV3Ethereum.COLLECTOR`) and each market's `distributionEnd` remain unchanged.
- **GHO sunset:** setting `maxEmissionPerSecond` to zero disables the reward distribution (the RewardsController cuts its `distributionEnd` to the current timestamp). Target Liquidity is set to 1 GHO because the RewardsController enforces a minimum of one whole token and rejects a value of zero; with emissions disabled this value has no economic effect.
- **GHO Deficit Offset:** increased from 115,000 GHO to 3,000,000 GHO, so that deficits are absorbed by the DAO before any slashing can occur while stakers unwind their positions and withdraw from the market. Once implemented, any future deficits generated by the GHO reserve are covered by the Treasury.
